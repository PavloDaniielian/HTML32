PROBLEM
STATEMENT
South Africa’s markets are abnormally concentrated and 31 out of 45 sectors recorded dominant firms. 70.45% of sectors have defined markets with dominant firms indicating high levels of static market concentration. Monopolization of all major sectors of the economy impacts:

• Market access,
• Creation and access to
employment,
• Growth and development of
SMMEs,
• Economic participation, and
• Consumption and pricing
PROPOSED
SOLUTION
The information, communication, and technology (ICT) sector is the most concentrated in South Africa with a Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) of 3 539.
Those who need ICT the most cannot access it because of price, lack of skills to navigate the digital world, and the absence of digital solutions geared for the lower LSM. The digital divide entrenches unequal market access.
Digital solutions provide the marginalized with the opportunity to leap-frog into the digital age and:
• Access infinite online resources and untapped markets,
• Grow networks, and
• Create their own solutions for personal development and success
